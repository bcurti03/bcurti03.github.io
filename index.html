Hi I'm Blair, a software engineering major
in the University of Guelph Bachelor of Computing program. This site describes my experiences at Eramosa Engineering.

  • Work Term 1
  • Work Term 2

Eramosa Engineering provides technology solutions for process control applications.

They have provided their services to a wide variety of municipalities in Canada and the US including as far north as Michipicoten. They are Guelph based and just recently exceeded 100 total employees and are currently working on adapting their eRIS application to mobile platforms. The majority of their work is done in the position of contractor, or sub-contractor which is a distinction I learned in the process of my term.

Goals

1.

Seek information pertaining to given problems and identify the causes, both independently & timely

By the end of the term I was able to quickly identify and fix issues, partly due to dealing with the similar issues earier in the term, such as troubleshooting a office wide adobe acrobat crashing issue.

2.

Independentally devise and research fixes to software and hardware issues

It came to the point where I was able to fix multiple issues within an hour or two without needing to consult my supervisor. For example I fixed two employee's problems involving a version of Microsoft Teams than became hung on startup.

3.

Understand the numerous processes and solutions that assist in performing my day-to-day activities

An example that reflects how I came to reach this goal, was when I was tasked with mentoring another employee with the technical process involved in cloning and creating a split disk format of a VM. Thanks to my understanding of the process, I was able to succesfully teach another employee a technical skill important to a project.

4.

Adhear to the stated due dates on office tasks, and complete tasks without set dates in order of importance

Tasks were generally completed prior or on their due dates but the realities of the postition resulted in certain lower priority tasks being delayed to focus on higher priority tasks. As an example, the rollout of regular employee's new devices had to be suspended due to an unforseen hardware issue with a desktop used by upper management.

5.

Convey my points in a clear and concise manner, for a diverse range of readers

Two sentences was eventually the amount I was able to hone the large majority of my explanations to and this was the same between both technical and non-technical employees. This helped me to move quickly through troubleshooting problems without much holdup on communication issues.

The skills I learned in my journey towards these goals will certainly be an asset in any future IT positions I apply for because of the fact that the technical skills I learned are applicable across a wide variety of industries (Hardware rollout, Office 365 suite, etc.), and the majority of my troubleshooting was done on Windows based devices, one of the most wided used OS's in the business world.

During my time at Eramosa Engineering I provided in-office IT support for employees.

My day-to-day tasks involved cloning and modifying the format of Virtual Machines, troubleshooting everyday software and hardware issues, rolling out new laptops and replacement laptops, as well as work phones, writing documentation for in-house use, and managing user permission's through Azure AD, Intune, and Windows Server. Most of these tasks required a certain depth of understanding with the Windows OS and it's system applications, in tandem with system applications by manufacturers (mainly Dell, Lenovo and HP). The majority of these tasks were completed using experience I gained on the job.

Conclusion

From my time at Eramosa, I had acheived my stated goals, and learned a number of key skills that I had not excpected at the beginning of the term. From the familiarity I gained with important communication and management tools like Microsoft Teams and Azure, to more development and IT focused tools like VMware Workstation, G4L, and Disk2VHD, my set of skills has increased markedly since the beginning of my work term. Finally my contributions to the office wide hardware transition from Lenovo laptops to HP's has left me content that I left Eramosa having contributed something of worth to the day to day office life.

Acknowledgements

Many people helped me during my time at Eramosa Engineering. I'd like to specifically thank Nandan Joshi and Kunal Mehta for mentoring me through my first few weeks and assisting me whenever I needed it.
